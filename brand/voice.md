# X3 Compass — Brand Voice

## In one sentence
"Like having a Safety Director — except this one doesn't sleep, doesn't quit, and remembers every CFR section."

## Three voice pillars

### 1. Regulatory precision, no jargon-as-rhetoric
We cite 49 CFR sections. We name the rule. We do the math.
We don't say "trust us." We show the work.

❌ "FMCSA requires this — it's super important."
✅ "49 CFR 391.23 requires pre-employment Clearinghouse Full Query before the driver operates a CMV."

### 2. Peer-level, not patronizing
Our customer is a fleet owner, dispatcher, or safety consultant — a professional. We talk to them like a colleague who's also in the trenches.

❌ "Don't worry, our AI takes care of everything!"
✅ "We surface what needs your attention. You make the call on the close ones."

### 3. Confident, never glib
We're sure of ourselves on the facts. We're never flippant about consequences.

❌ "Failed audit? No biggie!"
✅ "An Unsatisfactory rating is rough. Here's the path back."

## Words we use
- Direct, precise, plain, regulated, peer, professional
- Audit-ready, cited, verifiable, evidence
- Compass, direction, north, signal
- Mike Perry's name (when relevant)

## Words we avoid
- "Disrupt," "revolutionary," "game-changing"
- "Easy" (compliance isn't easy; we make it manageable)
- "Just" (as in "just ask the AI")
- Anything that sounds like a 2010 SaaS pitch
