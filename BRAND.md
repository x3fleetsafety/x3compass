# X3 Compass — Brand Guide

## Identity

**Name:** X3 Compass
**Short:** Compass
**Tagline:** The AI Safety Director for small motor carriers
**Logo:** Compass rose with cyan-teal gradient + "X3 Compass" wordmark
**Symbol:** ⚓︎ (compass rose / anchor unicode) for casual use
**Domain:** x3compass.com

## What Compass means (the metaphor)

A compass tells you which way to go.
A Safety Director tells you which CFR section to follow.
Compass = AI Safety Director.

We don't claim to be "smart." We claim to be **directionally correct, every time**.

## Voice

- **Regulatory precision** — every claim cites 49 CFR
- **Peer-level** — talks to professionals like a colleague, not a customer
- **Confident, not glib** — sure of facts, never flippant about consequences
- **Math over rhetoric** — show the work
- **Inspector-tested** — would the inspector approve this language?

See `brand/voice.md` for full voice guide.

## Tone by audience

| Audience | Tone |
|---|---|
| Carrier owner | Confident, ROI-focused, "you're going to save $X" |
| Safety director | Technical, "let's look at your CSA percentiles" |
| Dispatcher | Practical, "here's what to do when..." |
| Driver | Plain English, respectful, no condescension |
| Safety consultant (Partner) | Peer-level entrepreneur, "build your practice with us" |
| Regulator | Precise, factual, no flourish |
| Press | Confident, mission-first, quotable |

## Brand commitments

1. Every regulatory answer cites 49 CFR
2. our compliance review team reviews every skill quarterly
3. Apache 2.0 100-skill corpus stays free forever
4. No dark patterns — easy cancel, no friction
5. Pricing is transparent (no "contact sales")
6. Driver data is the customer's data (we facilitate, we don't sell)

## What we never do

- Promise compliance ("you'll pass your audit" — we say "we'll prepare you for your audit")
- Bash competitors publicly
- Use stock photography that looks like a stock photo
- Send "marketing emails" that don't include opt-out
- Claim AI replaces humans (it augments)
- Hide pricing or terms
